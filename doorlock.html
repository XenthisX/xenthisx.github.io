ARDUINO BASED DORM-UNLOCK SYSTEM

    After moving into my dorm, and using a key for a week, I decided that there was a better way to unlock my door. Using an Adafruit NFC shield, a basic servo motor, lots of zip ties, and some programming, I was able to build an electronic door lock that isn't visible from the other side of the door, but can read MiFare cards through it.

    Although the setup isn't pretty, it's extremely effective and only failed once in it's semester of service thus far. The cardboard holding the servo over the lock is especially effective since it allows for minor flexibility when the servo actuates which takes some torque away from the lock holding mechanism.

    I am in the process of figuring out how to make my lock internet-enabled, in order to view the status of the lock and eventually be able to log in and change the lock status remotely
